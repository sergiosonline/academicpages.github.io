Selected Projects and Presentations

  • Data Science
    • Analysis of Smoking Hazard and Socioethnographic Factors Influencing Smoking in the American Youth (Report)
      • Study of uptake rate of cigarette smoking, as well as risk of consuming other tobacco products, in a representative sample of American teenagers, surveyed throughout all states.
    • Bayesian Modelling of Data Correlated in Time and Space (Report)
      • Harnessing the INLA framework to fit a semi-parametric temporal model to examine CO2 emissions, and a spatial correlation model to examine the usefulness of satellite data in the study of Malaria incidence in Africa.
    • Fresh Over Rotten: The Value of a Critic's Pick in Hollywood (Report) (Presentation)
      • Study of how critical perception in Hollywood movies can translate to revenue differences, based on different features
  • Machine Learning
    • Attention-based Implementations of RNNs for Machine Translation Task (iPython Notebook)
      • Implementation of several attention-based architectures to solve a straightforward machine translation task, with a discussion of their merits and short-comings.
    • Exploration of the Model-Agnostic Meta Learning (MAML) Algorithm in a Regression Setting (iPython Notebook)
      • Fascinating algorithm which allows for straightforward transfer learning (task-to-task adaptation) with the only requirement being a model trainable by gradient descent.
    • MNIST Handwritten Digit Classification Problem *N<P* using MCMC (Report)
      • We use MCMC to overcome one of the most challenging hurdles in machine learning with reasonable success: When the number of your training cases is less than the number of parameters to be estimated.
  • Probabilistic (Monte Carlo) Computation
    • Bayesian Hierarchical Inference using Metropolis and Gibbs Sampling Algorithms (Report)
      • Fitting a Bayesian hierarchical model with fundamental MCMC algorithms to determine production differentials, studying the chain's convergence rate and properties
    • Implementations of an LCG Pseudorandom Generator, and Importance and Rejection Samplers (Report)
      • Exploration of probabilistic computation and pseudo-randomness
